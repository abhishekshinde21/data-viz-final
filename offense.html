Two Significant Factors of the Game: Goals and Assists

  • Please hover over each circle marker in the plot to get more information in the form of a tooltip.

To win a game in football, a team must score more goals than the opposing team. Manchester City (MC) did a lot of that this past season. Not only that, but they scored intelligently by producing numerous assists, which are player contributions to score goals. From the scatterplot above, it is evident that MC was the dominant offensive team with 82 goals and 55 assists. Not only that but this team averaged the highest goals per 90 minutes of 2.16. MC had a robust offensive lineup, including the playmaker Kevin De Bruyne, and new generational talents like Phil Foden, which contributed towards these results.


Other renown teams who finished in the top half of the standings, such as Manchester United and Liverpool, also showed good offensive statistics. These teams can be found in the top-right aspect of the scatterplot. Also, the chart shows a correlation between the number of goals scored and assists produced.
